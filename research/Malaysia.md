# Malaysia Market Research

## 1. Regulatory Environment
- **Import Regulations**: Import license required for certain categories; customs clearance via Malaysia Customs (KASTAM).
- **Mandatory Certifications**: SIRIM (Malaysian Standards), DOE (for energy‑efficient products), CE accepted.
- **Tariff Rates**: HS‑8 average 5‑10%; electronics 0‑5% under ASEAN‑Japan FTA.

## 2. Key Industries & Consumer Trends
- **Fast‑growing sectors**: Palm oil products, electronics assembly, automotive parts, halal food.
- **Top products**: Palm oil derivatives, semiconductor components, halal‑certified food products.
- **Consumer purchasing pattern**: Middle‑income growth driving demand for premium Korean brands; halal certification essential for food products.

## 3. Market Price & Competition
- **Typical price range**: Palm oil derivatives US$800‑1000 / ton; semiconductor components US$0.50‑2 per unit; halal food US$5‑10 per pack.
- **Major competitors**: Wilmar, Sime Darby in palm oil; local and Taiwanese electronics assemblers; local halal food brands.

## 4. Entry Barriers & Logistics
- **Buyer requirements**: Halal certification (JAKIM) for food products; local distributor with SIRIM compliance; GST registration.
- **Logistics infrastructure**: Port of Port Klang, Tanjung Pelepas; efficient highway system but congestion in KL area.
- **Additional notes**: Buyers prefer FOB or CIF; customs clearance 2‑4 days; halal certification process takes 2‑3 months.
